Everything You Need to Know About CGPA

Whether you're a college or university student, understanding CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is crucial. This blog post will help you understand what CGPA is, how to calculate it, and give you tips to maintain or improve it.

What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is the average of all grade points you earn during your academic program. Most universities use a 4.0 or 5.0 scale to evaluate student performance.

How to Calculate CGPA?

To calculate CGPA, follow these steps:

  • Convert your letter grades (e.g., A, B, C) into grade points.
  • Multiply each grade point with the course's credit hour.
  • Add all the total grade points together.
  • Divide by the total credit hours you have taken.

CGPA Formula

CGPA = Total Grade Points / Total Credit Hours

Tips to Improve Your CGPA

  • Attend classes regularly and take good notes.
  • Start preparing early for exams.
  • Focus on subjects with more credit hours.
  • Seek help when you're struggling in a subject.
